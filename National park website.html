Overwhelming mountains scenes with extraordinary wildlife creatures, all can be found in the Grand Teton National Park.

The park was originally built to preserve the Teton Range and several lakes at the foot of the mountains, until now, the total area of the Park has been expanded to 210,000 acres. There are different sections in the park, from the mountains to the lakes allow you to enjoy a large variety of activities with your family and friends. Knowing that the Grand Teton National Park is named after the Teton Range, visitors from all over the world can be joining our hiking trail and take a peek at this amazing scene created by our mother nature. What to do in Grand Teton
Hiking
Hiking in Grand Teton is fun but it can also be challenging due to the rugged nature of the landscape. Stop by our visitor center for weather conditions.
Camping
The backcountry is a great place to explore in the park throughcamping, however, advanced planning is needed.
Fishing
Many visitors come to enjoy this popular yet unforgettable fishing experience in Great Tteon National Park. Fishing activity is regulated by Wyoming state law and licenses are required.
Wildlife Viewing
Grand Teton is home to a wide variety of wild animals. Grizzly, black bear, moose , pronghor and many more live throughout the park. Always keep a safe distance when viewing wildlife!
Boating & Floating
There are different opportunities for visitors enjoying water in the park. Many more lakes are accessible for a variety of activities.
